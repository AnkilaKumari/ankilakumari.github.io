About Me

Hi, I’m Ankila Kumari. I’m from Bihar, India, and my journey into the world of GIS started after completing my undergraduate degree in Automobile Engineering. I worked as an Associate GIS Specialist at Cyient Pvt. Ltd., where I was part of projects involving satellite imagery, linear reference image (LRI), and spatial data analysis.

Now, I’m pursuing a Master’s degree in Geographic Information Science (MGIS) with a minor in Computer Science at the University of Minnesota. I also work as a Graduate Assistant at U-Spatial, where I support research using GIS.

Bridging Remote Sensing, GIS, and Real-World Impact

I’m passionate about using GIS to solve real-world problems especially in transportation and healthcare. I enjoy working with satellite imagery, remote sensing, and tools like Google Earth Engine (GEE) to understand patterns and make data more meaningful.

My skills include:

  • Satellite imagery analysis & remote sensing
  • linear reference image (LRI)
  • Google Earth Engine (GEE) for scalable, cloud-based geospatial analysis
  • Developing interactive dashboards and web maps
  • Writing code in Python, JavaScript (GEE), and working with APIs

What I love most about GIS is its ability to turn complex data into visual stories that help people make better decisions. Whether it’s mapping accessibility to healthcare or improving mobility systems, I enjoy building solutions that make a difference.

Outside of work and school, I love to travel, explore new cultures, try different foods, and find creative ways to use technology in everyday life.
